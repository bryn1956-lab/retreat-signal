# Research: Executive & Remote Team Retreat Directory

## User goal
Create a directory business with real monetization potential and minimize user effort by systematizing research, structure, and execution.

## Candidate angle selected
Executive retreats + remote worker / remote team retreats.

## Market observations
Competitors discovered in quick search:
- retreatsandvenues.com
- teamout.com
- theoffsiteco.com
- nextretreat.com
- venueretreat.com
- planretreat.com

Implication:
- This is not a blank market.
- Generic "retreat directory" is too broad.
- We need a differentiated wedge.

## Best differentiation options
1. U.S.-focused executive and leadership retreat venues
2. Offsite venues optimized for remote teams
3. Strong practical filters that planning teams actually care about
4. Region-first rollout (West Coast / Pacific Northwest / Mountain West)
5. Hybrid content + directory strategy

## Filters/features likely to matter
- nearest airport / drive time
- max sleeping capacity
- max meeting capacity
- exclusive buyout available
- wifi / AV / meeting rooms
- on-site food / catering
- outdoor activities
- all-inclusive or not
- price band
- ideal for exec retreats vs team bonding vs strategy offsites

## Monetization notes
Highest-probability early revenue:
1. Featured venue placements
2. Warm lead forwarding / inquiry routing
3. Concierge matching for teams that want help finding venues

Ads are not the primary model.

## Risks
- Data collection burden
- Existing competitors with larger inventories
- Need for trust and useful curation
- SEO will take time

## Go-to-market recommendation
Start narrower than "all U.S. retreats".
Recommended MVP angle:
- Executive and remote team retreat venues in the Western U.S.
- Focus on venues with lodging + meeting space
- Emphasize airport access, team size, and buyout suitability

## First build targets
- Define schema
- Define page architecture
- Build 25-50 seed venue records
- Publish location/category pages
- Publish 10 supporting planning articles
